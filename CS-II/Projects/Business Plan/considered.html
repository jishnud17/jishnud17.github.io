Considered ISPs


Automated Plantation
This ISP appealed to me because it seemed like an interesting idea to bring food to people's homes. I especially enjoyed how the food was ensured to be organic. The reason I didn't choose it however is the practicality. Having to grow the food as part of the isp seems impractical. Growing all the organic crops is a key component that doesn't seem viable.

TV360
This caught my eye because it seemed very unique. But I do not want to do this because it doesn't seem like something that people would buy. A spinning TV seems like it would take up more space than it would save. Additionally, this product does not seem like something that requires computer science.

Foob
I like the idea because it seems generally useful. It is a service that can be almost universally applied to anyone that is cooking. The reason I decided not to do it however is the fact that making an effective app like this would require hundreds of different combinations of ingredients. Additionally, apps like this already exist and this isn't adding anything new that would set it apart.

SSS Search
At first, I thought the idea of creating a secure browser seemed like a good idea. But then I realized that making a browser from scratch seems very difficult. Additionally, the proposal doesn't really discuss what the browser will do. Finally, the competition is trillion-dollar companies which makes it difficult to outperform them.

Symptoms Quiz
This one seemed interesting because it solves a problem that many people have. However similar to the isp that compiled ingredients I think realistically that it would be too difficult to compile all possible symptoms. Furthermore, many diseases and illnesses have common symptoms such as headaches or sore throats. Overall I'm dropping this one because it seems overly difficult and impractical.

Available Tables
This seemed interesting to think about and something that would make eating out more convenient. It seems like the app itself seems like something that could be done. I think however that getting permission and support from restaurants would be difficult to acquire. Additionally, this would make competing with other apps that already have restaurant support difficult. This is why ultimately I moved on from this ISP.

The Path
This project seemed intriguing because this was definitely a problem. Nevertheless, this is something that doesn't seem plausible because changing traffic lights isn't something that the government would allow. Emergency services do it rarely during situations that need it. I don't think students being late to class is something that the government would view as important enough to warrant being able to change lights. Overall this is a good idea but not one that can be implemented.

Project 1984
We choose to endorse our own project because we feel it has a valid marketing strategy, has a target audience in mind, and has a problem that we provide a good solution to. Our project idea is to implement RFID scanners into entrances of schools so that the schools can see who has an ID when entering the school and who doesn't. This ensures that people who don't go to the school can't enter undetected and that students without ID's are caught. This would replace the security guards that usually check the ID's. This would free up the labor and would ensure that people can't just sneak into the building in a large crowd and remain undetected. The project also isn't very costly because the RFID chips are very inexpensive and the scanners only need to be implemented in the entrances of the schools.

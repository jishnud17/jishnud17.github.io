This line graph for Bubble Sort shows the difference in efficiency between different lists of words. Bubble Sort goes thorugh the ordered graph very quickly as there is nothing to sort so it moves on after one pass. For reverse ordered, however, bubble sort times out at values of 50,000 words and above. This shows the weakness of bubble sort as a reverse ordered list would require many passes. Random Lists on the other hand take even longer as random lists would require more passes than reversed lists. This also times out at 100,000 words but suprisingly it works on lists of 50,000 words unlike reverse ordered lists. Bubble sort overall does very well on lists of up to 100 words completing them at a maximum of 7.4 milliseconds but starts taking longer as it moves on to lists of 1000 words. In conclusion, bubble sort is best used for smaller sets of words as opposed to larger sets.



The line graph of insertion sort is similar to bubble sort in that the ordered lists didn't take long to do because none of the elements needed to be moved. Unlike, bubble sort this time all 3 types of lists timed out at 100000 words. Additionally reverse-ordered also timed out at 50,000 words which is why Ihave a point for 30,000 words instead. The reverse ordered and random lines are very exponential with the time gratly increasing. Overall, insertion was more effective on random list than reverse ordered lists which can be explained by insertion sort's process: the sort moves each element before the elements it is lesser than. In reverse ordered lists it would have to move almost every element while in random lists there will be some elements that are already in the right position. In conclusion, Insertion sort is more efficent than bubble sort when it comes to longer lists but it is not suited for reverse ordered lists. When it comes to random lists insertion sort is more efficent than bubble sort or selection sort.

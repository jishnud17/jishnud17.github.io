Introduction

If everyone is moving forward together, then success takes care of itself, said indsustrialist Henry Ford. Humans are at their most optimal when working collaboratively. Almost all of the greatest wonders and creations have taken people working together towards a common goal. For example, it took 100,000 men working together to create the Great Pyramid at Giza. In my Computer Science class, we formed teams to work together toward creating a product that will benefit society. There may be decision biases that may adversely influence our team's decision making.

Bandwagon Bias

It is difficult to disagree with a large group in day to day life. This is especially true when working in a team. Bandwagon is defined in the dictionary as an activity, group, movement, etc. that has become successful or fashionable and so attracts many new people(2022). It essentially means going along wih plan or idea simply because many others also are going along with that plan. It can be intimidating to oppose a group, your own team members in particular. I think the best way to deal with this is to simply remind myself that as a member of the group I can share my opinion. This bias is very easy to get caught into, as when multiple team members get caught up in a shared idea it becomes very easy to unknowingly become apart of the group despite what you personally believe.

Convenience Bias

Sometimes instead of making decisions about what would make the final product better, we make decisions based on what would be easy. In AP Statistics, we learned about different types of sampling one of which was convenience sampling. This type of sampling is where the sampler picks the most easy sample group to interview although they may not be able to provide unbiased data. The same concept applies to the ISP team where the easiest idea will always seem appealing for the simple fact that it requires less work. According to the Principle of Least Effort or Zipf's law, people's actions are the expenditure of the least amount of effort to accomplish a task, or in other words people are simply lazy (Nordquist, 2019). To counter this, I remind myself of what my end goal is and that it isn't worth having a bad end product or one that could have been better just to avoid doing some extra work.

Ingroup Bias

When we need advice throughout our lives one of the first places we go to our friends. In a group when some of the group members are friends it is common to overlook other group members in favor of those you know. This can cause us to get caught up in assisting our friends and working less out of consideration for the good of the project or business as a whole (Sokolovski, 2020). It can also cause constructive opinions of other team members get ignored in favor of the the opinion of friends whose idea may be worse. This can cause resentment for being overlooked and an inability to accept new ideas. This can foster a negative working environment which can reduce productivity and collaboration. The best way to ward against this bias to be aware of other team members and make sure you evaluate their opinions objectively.

Sunk-Cost Bias

This bias goes hand in hand with convenience bias. It relates to Zipf's law and how people dislike doing a lot of work on things. When people do put effort in to an idea they become attached to it. This makes it difficult to accept the idea that the plan may be flawed and must be ultimately discarded. This bias can cause a lot of friction within a group due to difficulty accepting more efficient solutions to problems as the solution you have although flawed is difficult to give up on (Sokolovski, 2020). For example, when watching a movie even if it is boring, if you have already watched an hour of it you will stick through the movie in order for the time you have spent to not be wasted. To avoid this, remember the final goal and that if the final product isn't as good as it could be the time spent was wasted.

Overconfidence Bias

This is a person thinking that their ideas are superior to others and not considering the fact that they could be fallible.The main detriment of people like this is the usually having most of the team disliking them which is a direct cause to an increasingly negative work environment. Additionally, this bias can can pair with bandwagon bias and cause an entire team to overlook alternatives due to strong belief in their idea. Overconfidence in general, not limited to team collaboration, is harmful and can poorly affect any ventures an individual may pursue. The best way to deal with overconfidence is to simply challeng your ideas and the ideas of your group. Present alternatives or questions to make sure an idea is actually as good as it is believed to be.

Conclusion

Biases may be difficult and hindering to any cooperative effort, but they can be dealt with by keeping an open mind and always questioning ideas even your own.
